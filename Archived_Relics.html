    Cursed Relic: [Perspicacious Confluence]

    This relic has the appearance of a “helm”. It has been shaped out of some sort of quasi-metallic material, but upon further inquiry it is not metal at all. Instead, it is a composite material of several differing layers of unknown origin. With the right tools, one can see that these rather unworkable materials have been interwoven to differing degrees. How these metal-esque materials were turned into threads at all is unknown. Although, the resulting combination results in a moderately sturdy design which possesses a rather “unique” sheen. Light hitting its surface causes a bizarre “aurora” pattern to appear upon this relic.

    Though its composition is unique, [Perspicacious Confluence] derives its namesake from its other qualities. When fitted over a “head”, (most likely only one with a brain inside), the relic bolsters all aspects of the mind. Some examples would be enhanced mental clarity, memory recall, and critical thinking. Aside from this effect, [Perspicacious Confluence] also allows the user to sense the intentions and thoughts of those nearby.

     

    Subject [Svoyoss] has also written that [Perspicacious Confluence] protects against the mental degradation, strains, and mind-manipulations encountered when delving into the so-called “Abyss”. Without such a relic to reduce said strains upon those who enter this “Abyss”, they are likely to be “consumed”. It is implied that this is not a preferable outcome, and translating the text [Svoyoss] left has given a rough estimate of resisting these “strains” without a relic: approximately less than 0.1%.

    Illustrious Relic: [Scintillating Myriad]

     

    This is not one exact relic, but a collection of relics. Namely, a collection of “shards”. And this collection, in particular, shares the same characteristics with more complete shards despite the fact that all shards included under the name [Scintillating Myriad] are very small. Normally, shards of this size would be rather ineffective, finding it difficult to muster up the strength to simultaneously move themselves and extend their influence; however, [Svoyoss] found a use for them. By embedding them within his body, he was able to combine and centralize their power right where their defenses were needed the most.

    It is theorized that by embedding them within a “host”, these shards eventually attuned themselves to the host body’s life signatures. Over time, perhaps, they would come to recognize this pattern as their own. This may explain why [Scintillating Myriad] seems to proactively protect even the relics worn by [Svoyoss], even though they are not necessarily part of his body. It could be that they are able to sense their importance in keeping [Svoyoss] alive, and do not immediately destroy these aberrant objects despite their apparent danger to the subject.


    This recognition of a new whole may also explain why [Scintillating Myriad] is able to act more as a unit, rather than each shard lashing out on its own. Building off of this, it could be reasoned that the relic’s name is not referring to the shards in particular, but the unity between them.

    In case one could not infer the capabilities of such reduced shards, (since they are joined into a unison of sorts), they are unable to manifest the ruinous light. Additionally, they do not grant [Svoyoss] with the ability to take flight. Instead, this collection of shards is highly sensitive to direct threats upon its host body. Upon sensing an incoming projectile from some primitive weapon, or a collapsing wall which was neglected for too long, the shards project their force in the opposite direction, acting to keep the threat away from ending [Svoyoss].

    The strength of these unseen projections, however, always end up being extreme. As long as something large enough comes along and is poised to do harm, seemingly once the motions have been set into the threads by fate, then the shards act. Even the sad projectile known as an “arrow” will be met by enough force to topple a fully grown “tree”. This protection has caused more destruction than can be put into words. And though it has kept [Svoyoss] safe, it does not show any care for allies.

    However, through many efforts, the [Scintillating Myriad] eventually learned to hold back on its defensive reaction if the danger could not penetrate other relics used by [Svoyoss]. One notable example of this is the [Arms of Diminishing]. Most threats lost the necessary energy required to do harm before reaching the subject, resulting in a more than 50% reduction in violent “shard” reactions.

     

     

     

    Obscure Relic: [Arcane Strands]

    Meticulously locked within several sets of increasingly complicated boxes with similarly complicated locks, is a small, specially crafted bottle. Within this, is the relic known as the [Arcane Strands]. Whilst contained in its bottle, the relic seems to be more of a haze rather than strands. The cause of this is currently unknown, but it is speculated that when subjected to its own magics within a confined space, it changes its state of matter. Prior test show that the temperature and pressures within the bottle do not change while the [Arcane Strands] alter themselves.

     

    When taken out of its bottle, the relic radiates a peculiar light, and coalesces into the strands that are its namesake. However, it also radiates more than simple light. As seen on several occasions, exposure to the [Arcane Strands] in its strand-like state will burn the skin or carapace of anything which is touched by its light. Corpses are not immune to this relic’s trait, and even creatures who are undeterred by high heat sources will be burned regardless.

     

    Although it takes some time to be burned well enough to sustain lasting damage, such as severe scarring, the light from this strand phase can easily blind if left unchecked.

     

    Aside from these interesting traits, this relic also gives off a slime while it assumes its shape of countless strands. This slime, while exuded in scant quantities and requiring much care to scrape off of the strands safely, is of some worth. While its properties have not been fully studied, subject [Svoyoss] was able to concentrate this slime over a period of a year to create a ‘lure’.

    Parasite Relic: [Thread Intuition]

    There is little information on this “relic”. In truth, it seems less of a relic and more of a very useful parasite. Although, with its effect taken into account, it could easily reach relic status when applied to current models.

    When this parasite latches onto an eye, it will consume and replace it. If the host is strong enough to keep the parasite sustained, then it will become an “organ” capable of directly detecting the threads themselves. To give its host this ability, it directly links itself to the host’s brain using the connections the original eye had.

    The full list of effects upon the host when parasitized by this “relic” is not known. What little has been discerned is that the host’s new “eye” is highly sensitive to all “natural” light. [Svoyoss] seemingly coped with this effect by utilizing an item known as an “eyepatch”. (It is not known if the “eyepatch” hindered the parasite’s ability to see threads or not.) Additionally, the parasite links itself to the circulatory system of its host and leeches nutrients to sustain itself. This draws a great deal of energy from the host, and has left the subject [Svoyoss] crawling on the floor in delirium at times.

    However, based upon recovered texts, it is postulated that the parasite reduces its drain once it has the necessary energy to reproduce. This coincides with the onset of episodes of acute pain and weakness that afflicted [Svoyoss].

    An estimate of this reproductive cycle suggests that once a year, the parasite will subject the host to several unpleasantries.
